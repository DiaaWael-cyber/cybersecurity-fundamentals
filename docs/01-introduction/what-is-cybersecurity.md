# What is Cybersecurity?

## Overview
Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, programs, devices, and data from digital attacks, damage, or unauthorized access. It encompasses technologies, processes, and controls designed to safeguard digital assets and ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information.

## The CIA Triad - Core Principles

### Confidentiality
Ensuring that sensitive information is accessible only to authorized individuals, systems, or processes. This is achieved through:
- **Encryption**: Transforming data into unreadable formats
- **Access controls**: Authentication and authorization mechanisms
- **Data classification**: Categorizing data based on sensitivity

### Integrity
Maintaining the accuracy and completeness of data throughout its lifecycle. This involves:
- **Hash functions**: Verifying data hasn't been altered
- **Digital signatures**: Ensuring authenticity of digital messages
- **Version control**: Tracking changes to data and systems

### Availability
Ensuring information and systems are accessible when needed by authorized users. This includes:
- **Redundancy**: Backup systems and failover mechanisms
- **Disaster recovery**: Plans and procedures for system restoration
- **DDoS protection**: Mitigating denial-of-service attacks

## Types of Cybersecurity Threats

### Malware
Malicious software designed to harm or exploit systems:
- **Viruses**: Self-replicating programs that infect files
- **Ransomware**: Encrypts data and demands payment
- **Spyware**: Secretly monitors user activity
- **Trojans**: Disguised as legitimate software

### Social Engineering
Manipulating people into revealing sensitive information:
- **Phishing**: Deceptive emails or messages
- **Pretexting**: Creating fake scenarios to obtain information
- **Baiting**: Offering something enticing to lure victims

### Network Attacks
Targeting network infrastructure and communications:
- **Man-in-the-Middle**: Intercepting communications
- **SQL Injection**: Exploiting database vulnerabilities
- **Zero-day exploits**: Attacks on unknown vulnerabilities

## Cybersecurity Domains

1. **Network Security**
   - Firewalls and intrusion detection systems
   - VPNs and secure network architecture
   - Wireless security protocols

2. **Application Security**
   - Secure coding practices
   - Vulnerability testing and patching
   - API security measures

3. **Information Security**
   - Data protection policies
   - Encryption standards
   - Privacy regulations compliance

4. **Operational Security**
   - Incident response planning
   - Security awareness training
   - Business continuity planning

5. **Cloud Security**
   - Cloud infrastructure protection
   - Identity and access management
   - Data residency and sovereignty

## Importance of Cybersecurity

### For Individuals
- Protects personal information and identity
- Secures financial data and transactions
- Maintains privacy in digital communications

### For Organizations
- Protects intellectual property and trade secrets
- Ensures business continuity and reputation
- Maintains customer trust and regulatory compliance

### For Society
- Safeguards critical infrastructure (power grids, healthcare, finance)
- Protects national security interests
- Maintains stability in the digital economy

## Cybersecurity Best Practices

### Basic Hygiene
- Regular software updates and patches
- Strong, unique passwords and multi-factor authentication
- Regular data backups and system monitoring

### Organizational Measures
- Security policies and procedures
- Employee training and awareness programs
- Regular security audits and assessments

### Technical Controls
- Defense in depth strategy
- Principle of least privilege
- Security by design approach
